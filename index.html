Adaptive Fuzzy Predictor based Fast Terminal Sliding Mode Controller Design for Two-Link Robot Manipulator

Shreyas Kumar1, Bishesha Dash 1, Roshni Maiti1, Dipayan Guha2
1Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology, 2National Institute of Technology Allahabad

ICC 2024

Paper arXiv

Abstract

This paper proposes an Adaptive Fuzzy Predictor based Fast Terminal Sliding Mode Controller (AFP-FTSMC) for a two-link robotic manipulator operating under time-varying disturbances and model uncertainties. A Takagi–Sugeno fuzzy predictor estimates unknown, varying disturbances in real time, with adaptation laws derived via Lyapunov stability theory to guarantee boundedness of the estimation error. A fast terminal sliding mode control law then rejects the estimated disturbances, addresses chattering and singularity issues, and ensures finite-time convergence. Experimental validation on a custom two-link manipulator demonstrates that AFP-FTSMC outperforms conventional terminal sliding mode control in tracking accuracy, disturbance rejection, and smoothness of control signals.

How Does It Work?

  • Adaptive Fuzzy Predictor: A Takagi–Sugeno fuzzy system with Q IF–THEN rules models unknown disturbances d₁(t), d₂(t). Online adaptation via Lyapunov-based laws ensures estimation error \(\hat d(t)-d(t)\) converges.
  • Fast Terminal Sliding Mode Controller:

AFP-FTSMC reduces IAE by ~40%, suppresses chattering, and provides smooth torque profiles, with robust disturbance rejection under load variations.

Conclusion & Future Work

AFP-FTSMC combines rapid disturbance estimation with finite-time convergence, validated experimentally on a two-link manipulator. Future work includes energy-efficient adaptation and extension to multi-DOF systems.
